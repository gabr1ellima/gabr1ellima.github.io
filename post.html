Russian Alphabet

The Russian alphabet, also known as the Cyrillic alphabet, consists of 33 letters.

Vowels: а о э у ы и е ё ю я

Consonants: б в г д ж з й к л м н п р с т ф х ц ч ш щ

Voiceless Signs: ь ъ

Total: 33 letters

  • А а (A a) - Pronounced like "a" in "car."

    Example: адрес - adress

  • Б б (B b) - Similar to "b" in "bed."
  • В в (V v) - Similar to "v" in "very."
  • Г г (G g) - Similar to "g" in "go."
  • Д д (D d) - Similar to "d" in "dog."
  • Е е (Ye ye) - The sound is similar to "ye" in "yes."
  • Ё ё (Yo yo) - The sound is like "yo" in "yogurt."
  • Ж ж (Zh zh) - Similar to the "s" in "measure" or the "si" in "vision."
  • З з (Z z) - Similar to "z" in "zebra."
  • И и (I i) - Pronounced like "ee" in "see."
  • Й й (Y y) - Similar to "y" in "boy," but shorter.
  • К к (K k) - Similar to "k" in "kite."
  • Л л (L l) - Similar to "l" in "love."
  • М м (M m) - Similar to "m" in "mother."
  • Н н (N n) - Similar to "n" in "nice."
  • О о (O o) - Pronounced like "o" in "pot."
  • П п (P p) - Similar to "p" in "pen."
  • Р р (R r) - Rolled "r" sound, similar to the Spanish "r."
  • С с (S s) - Similar to "s" in "sun."
  • Т т (T t) - Similar to "t" in "top."
  • У у (U u) - Similar to "u" in "put."
  • Ф ф (F f) - Similar to "f" in "flower."
  • Х х (Kh kh) - Similar to the "ch" in the German "Bach" or the "j" in the Spanish "jalapeño."
  • Ц ц (Ts ts) - Similar to "ts" in "cats."
  • Ц ц (Ts ts) - Similar to "ts" in "cats."
  • Ч ч (Ch ch) - Similar to "ch" in "chat."
  • Ш ш (Sh sh) - Similar to "sh" in "shoe."
  • Щ щ (Shch shch) - Similar to "shch," a bit like the "sh" in "fresh cheese."
  • Ъ ъ (Hard sign) - Not pronounced on its own, but affects the preceding consonant.
  • Ы ы (Y y) - A unique sound, similar to the "i" in "bit."
  • Ь ь (Soft sign) - Softens the preceding consonant and is not pronounced on its own.
  • Э э (E e) - Pronounced like "e" in "bet."
  • Ю ю (Yu yu) - Similar to "yu" in "yule."
  • Я я (Ya ya) - Similar to "ya" in "yard."

Notes:

Note 1: There is no need to memorize the order of the alphabet. Learning the Russian alphabet means knowing the sound of each letter, that's all. The order doesn't matter. You need to see the letter and know how it is pronounced, what sound it conveys.

Note 2: The sound of some letters may change depending on the word in which that letter is placed. To avoid confusion, I suggest listening to how a native speaker or a teacher pronounces the words and memorizing the sound you heard. Reading as it is written can be confusing, so listen to someone pronouncing it, memorize the sound, and repeat it accordingly.
